LONDON 1883

The participants were Zukertort, Steinitz, Blackburne, Chigorin, Englisch, Mackenzie, Mason, Rosenthal, Winawer, Bird, Noa, Sellmann, Mortimer and Skipworth. Seven prizes of £250, £150, £120, £90, £70, £50 and £25 were available. The Löwenthal Consolation Prize of £50 was distributed among the losers, applying the Gelbfuhs system. Therefore, all players had some expenses covered.

Five games per week were played at the Victoria Hall in the Criterion from noon to 5 and 7-11 PM. A time limit of 15 moves was regulated by connected stop-clocks. When one clock was stopped, the other started running, and visa-versa. This design by Thomas Bright Wilson (with advice from Blackburne) was used in a tournament for the first time. Draws had to be replayed on Wednesdays and Saturdays. Only the third draw counted as half-a-point.

During the first cycle of the tournament, the players got a holiday for the Derby. That day platforms for visitors were erected at the Victoria Hall. As a result, one-hundred spectators could follow the game Steinitz-Zukertort during the second cycle. The tournament would last from 26 iv until 23 vi 1883. 

 

Notice the stop-clocks behind the board

 

Zukertort played an incredible tournament during twenty-three rounds. He scored twenty-two wins and one unlucky loss. Thereafter, he released the strain with opiates and lost the last three games. His dynamic style precedes Alekhine and Kasparov. He knows how to exert pressure on the opponent, his positional play is solid but not brilliant, and the combinations are exceptional. The famous win against Blackburne opens quietly, tension grows in the centre and the firework explodes suddenly.

Steinitz applied his uneven style. He took great risks against the strongest players and lost several games. Lesser players were met with his famous positional approach, and Steinitz crushed them with 17/20.

Blackburne played more cautiously than usual and scored many points with this approach. Chigorin knew how to exploit the weaknesses of Steinitz.

Mason occupied the second place during the first cycle. Eventually, he shared the fifth and sixth prize with Englisch and Mackenzie. The many repetitions of draws were unfavourable for Rosenthal. He made 26 valid scores in 45 rounds during the 59 days. A pleasant consolation was the brilliancy prize for a replay game with Black against Steinitz.

A historic incident occurred during the final banquet. A toast was made to the best player of the world. The crippled Steinitz tried to rise, but Zukertort was already standing. Steinitz remained seated with a red head. This matter was settled during the first world championship match in 1886. 

LONDON 1899

London 1899 was the fourth super chess tournament among Hastings 1895, Nürnberg 1896, Wien 1898 and Paris 1900. All important players were invited. Refusals came from Tarrasch who worked as a medical doctor and Charousek who suffered from a fatal illness. The businessman Burn had to withdraw at the opening’s day. Fifteen participants played double rounds from 30 v until 10 vii 1899, except for Teichmann. He had to withdraw after his encounters with Pillsbury, Blackburne, Chigorin and Tinsley due to an eye disease. His remaining games in the first cycle were declared as lost. 

Rounds were played in St Stephen’s Hall, near the Westminster Aquarium and Houses of Parliament. Seven tables, players and kibitzers seemed small in the gray hall. Statues of statesmen overshadowed them. The time limit was fifteen moves in one hour. Participants were entertained by the City of London chess club at Crystal Palace and the Star and Garter Hotel in Richmond, a favourite place of Dickens. A banquet took place in the International Hall of the Café Monico on 29 vi.

The double round tournament had a remarkable start. Janowsky won four games while Lasker only scored two points. The turnaround came in round ten, when the world champion defeated the leader. Thereafter it became a Lasker show. He ended 4½ points before the candidates Janowsky, Maróczy and Pillsbury after thirty rounds. The runner-up Schlechter became fifth. The world champion had increased his superiority over the candidates since Nürnberg 1896. Strong players like Marshall, Marco and Mieses had to play in the single round tournament. Marshall won. An amount of £1020 for prizes and consolation money was distributed on 11 vii 1899. Lasker got £250 and a gold medal. Steinitz won no prize for the first time in his life. He died in poverty the next year. 

The event has been presented in the tournament book by Hoffer and the chess journals of the day. This information is difficult to obtain for most chess enthusiasts. Therefore I made a generous selection of thirty-one games due to the importance of London 1899. 

 

LONDON 1922

A super tournament was played in the Central Hall of Westminster from 31 vii until 19 viii 1922. World champion Capablanca participated in his first tournament after he had defeated Lasker. He won by 13/15 before Alekhine, Vidmar, Rubinstein, Bogoljubow, Réti, Tartakower, Maróczy and Yates.  Euwe made a fine debut. 

The world champion had set up a protocol for the world championship. It was discussed after the tournament. A challenger had to raise $10,000 for a match in the main rule. Alekhine, Bogoljubow, Maróczy, Réti, Rubinstein, Tartakower and Vidmar signed the protocol. Rubinstein and Nimzowitsch failed to breach the 'golden wall'. Alekhine succeeded and also took the title in 1927.
