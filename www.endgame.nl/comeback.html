EUWE'S COMEBACK
                                                                 Jan van Reek

 pgn-file of Euwe's comeback       CB-file of Euwe's comeback

When Alekhine died on 24 iii 1946, the chess world championship became vacant. Former champion Max Euwe became a main candidate. We follow his achievements. 
His first test was organised by the Maastrichtsche Schaakvereninging (MSV) from 20 until 29 iv 1946. Ten men took part in the master group. The club had its 25th Anniversary in 1945. A Jubilee tournament happened in the next year. Euwe won the event by 7½/9.

A second test happened in June 1946. The 'Zaandamsche Schaakclub' celebrated its 20-years Anniversary in 1944 with a small chess event. A bigger Jubilee happened in the Zaanstreek from 3 until 14 vi 1946. Twelve men played the 'Internationaal Schaaktournooi Zaanstreek'. Euwe won by 9½/11.
An international was organized at the 75-years Jubilee of the chess club Staunton in Groningen. It was a difficult enterprise after World War II. Only Keres, Fine and Reshevsky were missing from the elite. Nineteen rounds were played from 13 viii until 7 ix 1946. The final standings became: Botvinnik 14½/19 (f1500), Euwe 14 (f1250), Smyslov 12½, Najdorf and Szabo 11½, Boleslavsky and Flohr 11, Lundin and Stoltz 10½, Denker, Kotov and Tartakower 9½, Kottnauer 9, Yanofsky 8½, Bernstein and Guimard 7, Vidmar 6½, Steiner 6, O´Kelly 5½, Christoffel 5. Botvinnik had won the event. Euwe was the only one who had given him a stiff competition. Max had become a professional chess player during the event.

Euwe remained successful for half-a-year. He won the small tournaments Amsterdam 1946 and Leiden 1946. Max crushed Grob in the match Zürich, February 1947.
Mar del Plata 1947 was played in March. He began the tournament by a win against Najdorf and scored 5 points in 6 rounds. The turning point happened in the game versus Julio Bolbochan. Euwe also lost in the hands of the outsider Hounie Fleurquin during the next round and did not compete for the first place anymore.
Max had changed from a world-class player to an average grandmaster. Causes were an overdose of chess and the effect of aging. Euwe failed in the world championship of 1948. Botvinnik had no competition and win the tournament of his life.
