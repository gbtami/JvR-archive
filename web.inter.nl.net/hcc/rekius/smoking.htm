NO BASIS FOR CAMPAIGNS AMONG CHILDREN

+ + + The onset of smoking is unpredictable!! + + +

One night I carried out a logistic regression on a sample of schoolchildren. Who started smoking from the age of 13 to 15 was predicted with the accuracy of the roll of a die! Six factors (smoking by age peers, parental smoking, availability of tobacco, pocket money, self-efficacy and beliefs) were statistically significant predictors over two years but failed to classify new smokers accurately. 

Dutch scientists have critical views on simplistic smoking mechanisms. My predecessor Gadourek refuted Freud's oral fixation, because he found no relationship between tobacco and candy consumption. He used bivariate analysis as a sharpshooter. I applied multivariate analysis as a machine gun on hypothetical mechanisms. 

The results were published in the proceedings of a world congress on 'Tobacco and Health' in 1995, since I had paid the conference fees. Common scientific reactions were unwillingness and plagiarism, because my findings hit at the basis of primary smoking prevention. Facts conflicted with vested interests of social psychologists. 

Jan van Reek

Smoking trends in the Netherlands and Britain
