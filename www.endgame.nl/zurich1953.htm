NEUHAUSEN / ZÜRICH 1953

 

Fifteen players participated in the candidates’ tournament of 1953. Kotov, Petrosian, Taimanov, Geller, Averbakh, Gligoric, Stahlberg and Szabo had qualified in Stockholm 1952. Bronstein, Boleslavsky, Smyslov, Keres and Najdorf were the best in the previous candidates’ tournament. Reshevsky and Euwe had participated in the world championship of 1948.

Eight rounds were played in the Kirchgemeindehaus in Neuhausen am Rheinfall from 30 viii until 12 ix and the remaining twenty-two rounds in the Kammermusiksaal of the Kongreßhaus in Zürich from 13 ix until 23 x 1953. So thirty rounds were played in nearly two months.

Ten Soviet ‘state amateurs’ took part. Their domination was challenged by Euwe in the first three rounds. Later Reshevsky became an important competitor.

Smyslov won the tournament in a grand style. His score against his main competitors was brilliant. He challenged Botvinnik. Their match ended with 12–12 in 1954. Reshevsky had the best score against the weaker players but he only achieved ½/4 against Smyslov and Bronstein. The newcomers in the world championship race, Petrosian and Geller, had to play in the semi final and final of the Soviet championship in 1951, Stockholm 1952 and this tournament, a total of 84 games!

ZÜRICH 1959

 

Ten foreign and six Swiss combatants played in Zürich’s Kongreßhaus from 18 v until 8 vi 1959. We follow the race between the future world champions Mischa Tal and Bobby Fischer. 

Mischa did not take the six Swiss players seriously, but defeated five of them. His win against Nievergelt was highly entertaining. When he tried to treat Gligoric similarly, he lost quickly. Bobby scored poorly at the end. Tal won the event before Gligoric and Fischer. The players got 60 Francs for a win, 25 for a draw and 10 for a loss. So Tal earned 695 Swiss Francs.
