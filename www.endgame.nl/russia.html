The Russian Chess Championship started, when Schiffers defeated Chardin in St Petersburg (+5,-4 in 1874). Chigorin became the next champ in 1879.
Chigorin did not defend the title for two decades. 'All Russian Champion' became at stake in a biannual tournament at the end of the nineteenth century. Chigorin and Rubinstein won most events.
Forty-four Russian championships were played during the Soviet period. Later the first two were regarded as Soviet championships. The next events lacked the participation by citizens from Moscow and Leningrad.
When the tournament was resumed during the modern times, all citizens could take part. The first modern Russian championship happened in Orel during the autumn of 1992. Eleven Swiss rounds were carried out. Kirsan Ilyumzhinov, president of Kalmykia, restored the chess championship by financial support in 1994. Svidler won several events. Later the level went down again. Zhukov gave a new impulse to the tournament. A super-final became Moscow 2004. The event happened in the Central Chess Club from 2005 until 2012. Kasparov participated once in the closed event. When the tournament went to Nizhny Novgorod, Svidler won again. Kramnik was less successful.
