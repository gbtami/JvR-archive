SOFIA 2005

pgn-file of Sofia 2005 (30 games)    zip-file of CBase-files of Sofia 2005

 

Six grandmasters played double rounds in the Grand Hotel Sofia from 12 until 22 v 2005. Organiser was Silvio Danailov, Topalov’s manager. MTel sponsored. Games had to be played until the end. Draws were only allowed by the arbiter in the case of a repetition of positions or theoretical draw. 

 

Grand Hotel Sofia

Co-leaders Topalov and Ponomariov at the start of round 9

 

Kramnik and Adams led by 3/5, Anand and Ponomariov had 2½/5, Topalov and Polgár trailed by 2/5 points at the end of the first cycle. Topalov produced the incredible score of 4½/5 in the second cycle. Kramnik and Adams only made two draws. Veselin Topalov won the tournament overall by his fantastic finish. He was well prepared and fought brilliantly.

The allotted time for a game was two hours for forty moves, followed by one hour for twenty moves and half-an-hour for the rest of the game. Most battles lasted about six hours. Extra rest by short draws was prohibited. Two players were unable to play ten games in eleven days. Kramnik and Adams broke down during the second cycle. 

Topalov remains concentrated during the whole game. Nowadays, a lot of energy is needed for a modern tournament of classical chess. As preparation, Veselin trains chess for 6 to 7 hours per day. This might easily lead to a burnout. Therefore a psychologist counsels him regularly.
