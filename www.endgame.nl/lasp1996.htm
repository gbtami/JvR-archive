LAS PALMAS 1996

Top chess has been played in Las Palmas since the match Larsen-Uhlmann in 1971, a quarter final of the candidates’ matches. Super tournaments were won by Timman in 1981 and Kamsky in 1994. Anand and Kamsky played a candidates' match in 1995.

An even bigger event took place from 9-21 xii 1996. The six best players of the world participated in a competition with double rounds. A highlight was the confrontation between Kasparov and Karpov, the world champions. Super Torneo Mundial de Ajedrez was played in CICCA, the cultural centre of Las Palmas.

The tension was high during the tournament. Prudent play led to draws. A great game was Anand - Ivanchuk in round three. Vishy sacrificed an exchange for pressure in the endgame. He won and celebrated his birthday. Much attention drew the games between Kasparov and Karpov.

The tournament was a big success for the PCA. Kasparov won and Anand became second. Both players defeated Karpov. The last place was a strange result for the FIDE champ.
