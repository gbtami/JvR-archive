NOTTINGHAM 1936

Nottingham 1886 was won by Burn. A young man triumphed in a lesser group. Fifty years later, he had become Councillor Derbyshire, President of the British Chess Federation. He guaranteed half of the costs for an international tournament. The other half was raised in a campaign by Rees, Stevenson and Sir Thomas.

The participants stayed at Victoria Station Hotel and partook in a garden party at Rempston Hall. Eleven foreigners and four Englishmen played at Nottingham University from 10 until 28 August 1936. Games began in the afternoon. The time limit was 36 moves in two hours. It should have been 30 moves, but a misprint was left unchanged. Adjourned games were resumed in the evening.

The games between the eight best players have been analysed anew. The top eight were champion Euwe, former champions Lasker, Capablanca and Alekhine, Botvinnik, Fine, Reshevsky and Flohr. 

 

Standing: Fine, Tartakower, Vidmar, Bogoljubow, Tyler, Alexander, Flohr, Reshevsky, Botvinnik, Mackenzie (arbiter)

Seated: Thomas, Lasker, Capablanca, Derbyshire, his wife, Euwe, Alekhine, Winter

 

Capablanca enjoyed the Indian Summer of his career. He had won Moscow 1936 and shared the first place in Nottingham. He studied openings during the event! Botvinnik played for the second time in England. His success was highly praised in the Pravda. The feelings in the West were mixed, because the show process against Zinoviev and Kamenev had started during the tournament. A photo of Botvinnik, Capablanca and Lasker, having a nice cup of tea, made a pleasant impression, but the blossoming romance between Mr. Stevenson and Miss Menchik was a bigger story in Nottingham. 

Euwe, Fine and Reshevsky followed by half-a-point, Alekhine by one point, and Flohr and Lasker by one-and-a-half point. It would be Lasker’s last important tournament. Other chess stars would continue with close competitions in the thirties.
