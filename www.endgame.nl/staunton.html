Staunton Chess Events and London Classics pgn         CB-files Staunton Chess Events and London Classics
The Staunton Centenary started on 26 v 1951, exactly 100 years after London 1851. Sixteen players from Western Europe and Yugoslavia participated. The event happened in three English cities. Gligoric won.
Samuel Reis's Cigar Divan was opened in 1828. Its name became Simpson's Grand Divan Tavern in 1848. The 175th anniversary was celebrated in August 2003. Four Englishmen played double rounds. The event was named after Howard Staunton, the strongest chess player during the 1840s. Speelman won.
The second Staunton Memorial was played from 23 until 29 viii 2004 at Simpson's-in-the-Strand in London.
Staunton Memorial three was played in London from 19 until 30 viii 2005. The number of participants was increased from four to six.
The fourth Staunton Memorial happened in London from 14 until 25 viii 2006. Six rounds were played in Simpson's-in-the-Strand. The last five rounds were played in Wellington College at Crowthorne outside London. Jan Mol was the new sponsor. The event became an encounter between six British and six Dutch players, as a consequence.
The fifth Staunton Memorial occurred in Simpson's-in-the-Strand from 7 until 18 viii 2007. Again six British and six Dutch players participated. They were lodged in the Staunton Hotel.
The sixth Staunton Memorial happened in Simpson's-in-the-Strand from 7 until 18 viii 2008. Again British and Dutch players battled in London.

The seventh Staunton Memorial was played at Simpson's-in-the-Strand from 8 until 17 viii 2009. Five British and five Dutch players carried out double rounds, according to the Scheveningen System. Short, Howell, McShane, Jones and Adams battled versus Smeets, Van Wely, Sokolov, L'Ami and Werle.

A Staunton Memorial Dinner was organised in 2010.

LONDON CHESS CLASSICS

When the Staunton Memorial events were finished, a new large tournament was started. Again British players encountered foreigners, but there were important differences: 1. Four Englishmen met four grandmasters from the rest of the world. 2. The event happened in December. 3. Quick draws were not allowed and soccer scores were produced. 4. Bankers gave a support of 120,000 euro.
London Chess Classic 1 was played at the Olympia Conference Centre from 8 until 15 xii 2009. Final standings: Carlsen 13/7, Kramnik 12, Howell and Adams 9, McShane 7, Ni Hua and Nakamura 6, Short 5. Carlsen became number one in the next elo-list.

London Chess Classics number two was played from 8 until 15 xii 2010. The prize fund increased to 145,000 euro. Final standings: Carlsen 13, McShane and Anand 11, Kramnik and Nakamura 10, Adams 8, Howell 4, Short 2.

The third London Chess Classic was played from 3 until 12 xii 2011. Four Englishmen encountered five foreigners. The final standings became: Kramnik 16/8, Nakamura 15, Carlsen 14, McShane 13, Anand and Aronian 9, Short 6, Howell 4, Adams 3.

The fourth London Chess Classic was played from 1 until 10 xii 2012. Three Englishmen encountered six foreigners. Carlsen reached a record rating of 2861. The final standings became: Carlsen 18/8, Kramnik 16, Nakamura and Adams 13, Anand 9, Aronian 8, Polgar 6, McShane 5, Jones 3.

The fifth London Chess Classic was played from 11 until 15 xii 2013. Sixteen grandmasters carried out rapid games. Eventually Nakamura became the winner.
