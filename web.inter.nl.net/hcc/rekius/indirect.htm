The present fall in smoking by Dutch adolescents

                                                              Jan van Reek

 

Daily smoking among schoolchildren, aged 10-19, remained constant at 19% from 1992 until 2002. Then it dropped to 11% in 2012.

 

Table 1. Changes in daily smoking among schoolchildren, aged 10-19

1992-2002    0%

2003-2004   -4%

2005-2008    0%

2009-2012   -4%

(intervals of reliability = 1%)

 

The first decrease coincides with the tobacco advertisement ban in 2003 and the excise duty increase in 2004. Later the modification concurs with the excise duty increases in 2008 and 2011. No change occurs when no policy measures are taken. Policy measures and adolescent smoking correlate perfectly.

Crucial anti-smoking measures have been taken in the Netherlands since 2003. A ban on advertisement and excise duty increases lower the smoking by adolescents. The effects are surprisingly strong.

 

The information about adult smoking can be regarded as data about daily smoking. So adult smoking is comparable with daily smoking by adolescents (see table 2).

 

Table 2. Percentages of daily smokers by age from 1992 until 2011

 age 10-12 13-15 16-19 20-34 35-49 50-64 65+

1992   0    14    36    38    40    34   23

2002   0    14    35    35    37    28   16

2011   0    10    25    29    27    26   13

(surveys: TNS NIPO)

 

A substantial decrease of smoking in 1992-2002 is only found for persons of 50 and over (as a result of smoking cessation). Nowadays, smoking also decreases a lot at lower ages.

The government has planned an excise duty increase and a higher age of 18 years to purchase tobacco in the Netherlands for 2013. Likely, adolescent smoking will go down further. It might halve in twelve years!
